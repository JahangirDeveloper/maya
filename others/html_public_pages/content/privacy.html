Overview

This Privacy and Cookie Policy explains how Jamaliki uses your personal information. We are committed to protecting the privacy of our users and customers. This privacy policy is self-explanatory. It will inform you how we gather, define, and use your personal information, such as your name, email address, address and other contact details. 
This policy (together with our Terms & Conditions) sets out the basis on which any personal data we collect from you, or that you provide to us, will be processed by us.
Please read the following carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your personal data and how we will treat it, and decide whether and how you wish to use our site, products and services. 

Information we may collect from you

As you navigate through and interact with our Website, we may automatically collect certain information (User Data) about your equipment, browsing actions and patterns, including:
•    Personal information that you choose to share with us when you register for services, emails, newsletters and alerts
•    If you contact us, we may keep a record of that correspondence.
•    Information about the way our website has been used by you and others – collected on an aggregate basis.
•    We might also collect your IP address through the placement of cookies. 
It is important that the Personal Information you give us when you register as a member or when you use our website, is correct and accurate. This includes, for instance, your correct contact (including e-mail) or details at all times.
We keep personal information secure. We strive to secure personal data in our possession wherever possible. However the transmission of information via the internet is not completely secure. So, while we do our best to protect your Personal Information, we cannot guarantee the security of your data transmitted to our website. Any transmission is at your own risk. Once we have received your Personal Information we will use strict procedures and security features to prevent unauthorized access. No company can promise you perfect security, but we want our users to know that securing our data is extremely important to us and that developing meaningful security mechanisms is a priority for Jamaliki.


How do we use the information you provide to us?

Generally speaking, we use personal information for purposes of administering and expanding our business activities, providing customer service and making available other products and services to our customers and prospective customers. Sometimes, we may also use the information we collect to notify you about important changes to this website, new services and special offers we think you will love. You may notify us of your desire not to receive these offers by clicking the unsubscribe link contained in each such email terms. Please note that this will only be done if you have consented to receiving such communications at the point of registration. 
Unsubscribe from our regular emails
You can opt out of receiving automatic updates to the latest news by disabling updates at any time by clicking on the Unsubscribe link in the footer of the email you have received. Alternatively, you may email us. Please allow 48 hours for your email address to be removed from our system.
Our policy on Cookies
Similar to other websites, Jamaliki uses a standard technology called “cookies” (see explanation below, “What Are Cookies?”) and Web server logs to collect information about how this site is used. Information gathered through cookies and web server logs may include the date and time of visits, the pages viewed, time spent at this site, and the websites visited before and just after our website. The information we collect automatically is statistical data, and does not identify any individual. It helps us to improve our Website to deliver a better and more personalized service. 


We use cookies to:

 

•    Contact you by email, mail, telephone or text message to let you know about any new products, services or promotions provided by third parties, but only according to the permissions you give us 
•    Store information about your preferences, allowing us to tailor our Website according to your individual interests
•    Collect data about your use of the website which is then anonymized and used to help us improve our online services 
•    Make our website work as efficiently as possible.
•    Speed up transactions and administer services to you and to our partners.

 

What is a cookie?


“A cookie is a small file, typically of letters and numbers, downloaded on to a device when the user accesses certain websites. Cookies are then sent back to the originating website on each subsequent visit. Cookies are useful because they allow a website to recognize a user’s device.”
— From the Information Commissioner’s Office guidance on the use of cookies
Find out more about the use of cookies on www.cookiecentral.com

How do we use information we collect from cookies?
Cookies do not contain any personal information about you, but personal information that we store about you may be linked, by us, to the information stored in and obtained from cookies. As you browse our site, the site uses its cookies to differentiate you from other users to prevent you from seeing unnecessary advertisements or requiring you to log in more often than necessary. Add to that, we use the collected information, which does not identify individual users, to analyze trends, to administer the web site, to track users' movements around the site and to gather demographic information about our user base as a whole. Cookies, together with our web server's log files, will allow us to calculate the aggregate number of people visiting our website and to determine which parts of the site are most popular. This helps us gather information to assess and understand customer feedback and identify usage hotspots.

Third Party Cookies
Third party cookies are placed by or on behalf of independent advertisers or partners who are advertising on our site. Most of the cookies we use are ours, however, we issue sometimes a cookie by a third party to help us operate the site -- these are sometimes known as “web beacons” or “clear gifts”. They are anonymous – they cannot identify individuals. They are used for statistical analysis by allowing the advertiser or partner to count how many people have seen their advertisement or offers. They might also allow the advertiser or partner to tailor advertising to you when you visit other websites.
We may disclose general, aggregated or anonymized data about your visits and use of our website, but we will never disclose any of your Personal Information which identifies you or from which you may be identifiable, for instance we will never disclose your contact details including but not limited to your phone number, address, name and email address.
Third parties that usually generate these cookies have their own, very strict, privacy policies; however, we don't have access to these cookies. Other than allowing them to be served, we have no role to play in these cookies at all, although we may use statistical information arising from these third party cookies to enhance our website experience.
You can opt out of interest-based ads by Google through Ads Settings. When you opt out, you will still see ads but they may not be related to factors such as your interests, previous visits to other websites, or demographic details.

How do I remove or block cookies?
If you wish to restrict or block web browser cookies which are set on your device then you can do this through your browser settings; the Help function within your browser should tell you how However, please note, that switching off cookies may restrict your use of the website and/or delay or affect the way in which it operates.

We strongly recommend all users to post anonymously on our website. We cannot be held responsible for the use of such information by such third parties.
